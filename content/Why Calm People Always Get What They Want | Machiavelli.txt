It is about time.

Here are the six principles of the calm person, as Machiavelli would have described them.

First. Emotion is information, not instruction. Feel it. Do not be controlled by it. The moment someone or something can predictably make you angry, afraid, or desperate, they have power over you. Protect your emotional response the way you protect anything else valuable.

Second. Be the fox before you are the lion. Prepare before you act. Think before you speak. Understand the terrain before you charge across it. The person who understands the situation controls the situation.

Third. Build the dam in the sunshine. Prepare for the storms of life during the calm times. Build your financial stability, your relationships, your reputation, your skills, your health — all of it — when you do not need to. So that when the flood comes, you are not scrambling.

Fourth. The appearance of calm creates the reality of power. When you are visibly steady, people trust you more, offer you more, and follow you more readily. This is not manipulation. It is the natural byproduct of actually being someone worth trusting.

Fifth. Play the long game. The thing you want five years from now is being built or not being built by the decisions you make today. The person who can sacrifice short-term comfort for long-term advantage — consistently, patiently, without bitterness — will almost always end up in a stronger position than the person who could not wait.

Sixth. Pay attention. Put down the noise of your own emotional reactions and actually watch what is happening around you. People tell you everything you need to know about them if you are calm enough to receive the information.
